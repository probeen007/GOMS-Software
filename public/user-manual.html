Operations & User Manual
Version: v2.0.0 | Release Date: July 2026 | Target: Internal Garage Staff

Welcome to the PM Auto Mobiles Garage Operations Manual. This document details the end-to-end operation of the web application (GOMS — Garage Operations Management System), highlighting roles, operational flows, and step-by-step procedures to manage your garage digitally. You can always reopen this manual by clicking the ? help icon in the top navigation bar of the dashboard.

What's New in v2.0.0

The separate Quotation and Job Card modules have been retired and merged into a single, simpler Servicing workflow. Invoices now support a VAT / Non-VAT toggle at generation time, direct-counter (PC) billing for walk-in parts sales, a Customer Dues report with month/year filtering, manually-set service reminders with WhatsApp follow-up, and all dates across the system now display in the Nepali Bikram Sambat calendar.

Core Operational Journey (End-to-End Workflow)

This section provides step-by-step instructions on how to take a customer vehicle through the entire garage lifecycle, from booking to payment.

Step 1: Appointment Booking

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Admin

  1. Navigate to the Appointments page using the left-hand sidebar menu.
  2. Click the blue Book Appointment button in the upper-right corner. This opens a booking form.
  3. Search for the customer by name or phone. If they are new, add their name, phone, and vehicle details directly in the flow (or from the Customers & Vehicles page first — vehicle model, plate number, and vehicle number can be captured there too).
  4. Set the appointment date & time and the assigned technician.
  5. Add any intake comments or customer complaints (e.g., "Squeaking sound when brakes are applied").
  6. Click Book Appointment to save it.

Step 2: Vehicle Intake & Check-In

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Admin, Technician

  1. When the customer arrives, open the appointment list and click Check-In on their booking.
  2. Record the odometer reading and any pre-existing damage notes/photos if applicable.
  3. The appointment status updates to checked-in, and the vehicle is ready to move into Servicing.

Step 3: Servicing — Parts, Labour & Work Tracking

Primary Roles: Technician, Receptionist, Admin

  1. Navigate to the Servicing page and create a new servicing record for the customer's vehicle.
  2. Add Parts used in the repair — selecting an inventory item and quantity automatically decrements live stock levels. If stock drops below the configured minimum, the item is flagged and admins/accountants are alerted.
  3. Add Labour lines describing the work performed and the cost/hours involved.
  4. Document repair notes as work progresses.
  5. When the vehicle is ready, record the Mileage Out and click Close to mark the servicing record as closed.
    One Invoice Per Record

    A servicing record can only be invoiced once it is closed, and each closed record can only be linked to a single invoice — the system blocks duplicate billing automatically.

Step 4: Generating the Invoice (VAT / Non-VAT)

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Accountant, Admin

  1. Navigate to the Invoices & Payments page and click the Generate button.
  2. Select the closed, not-yet-invoiced servicing record from the list.
  3. Choose the invoice type by toggling VAT (tax applied at the configured rate, default 13%) or Non-VAT.
  4. Optionally set the Next Service Date right here if you already know when the vehicle should return — this field is entered manually by staff (it is no longer auto-calculated), and can also be set or changed later when recording payment. Leaving it blank is fine.
  5. Click Generate Invoice. The invoice is created with the parts/labour totals, VAT (if applicable), and is ready for payment.

Step 4b: PC Direct Billing (Counter Sales, No Work Order)

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Accountant, Admin

For walk-in customers buying parts over the counter without a full servicing work order:

  1. On the Invoices page, click PC Billing.
  2. Select the customer, and optionally link a vehicle (for odometer tracking and service reminders).
  3. Add line items (parts or misc. charges) with quantity and unit price, and an optional discount.
  4. Choose VAT or Non-VAT, optionally enter the odometer reading and Next Service Date if a vehicle is linked, then submit. The invoice is generated the same way as a servicing invoice and supports A4 half-size print output for quick counter receipts.

Step 5: Billing, Settlements & WhatsApp Receipts

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Accountant, Admin

  1. Open the target invoice from the Invoices list — unpaid balances are shown with clear status badges.
  2. Click Record Payment, enter the amount, and select the payment method: Cash, Card, Fonepay, or Bank Transfer. Add a reference note if applicable.
  3. Loyalty Redemption: if the customer has redeemable points, you can apply them as a discount directly on the invoice.
  4. You can set or update the Next Service Date here too, and toggle Send WhatsApp to open a pre-filled WhatsApp message with the payment confirmation for the receptionist to send manually.
  5. Submit the payment. The invoice status updates automatically (unpaid → partially-paid → paid).

Step 6: Loyalty Points Credit

Primary Role: System Automated

  • When an invoice becomes fully paid, the system automatically awards loyalty points at a rate of 1 point for every Rs. 10 of the invoice total, recorded in the Loyalty Ledger.

Step 7: Service Reminders & Manual WhatsApp Follow-up

Primary Roles: Receptionist, Admin

  1. Any invoice with a Next Service Date set appears under Appointments → Service Reminders, sorted soonest-due first, with an "Action Required" flag when due within a day.
  2. Click Send to WhatsApp on a reminder card — this opens a pre-filled WhatsApp message to the customer's phone number, which staff review and send manually (this is not a fully automated background send).
  3. After sending, the card is marked Reminder Sent but stays in the list — it is not removed automatically. When you're done with it, click the Delete button on that card to remove it from the worklist yourself. Deleting a reminder only clears the reminder date; it does not affect the invoice or payment record.

Management & Support Modules

1. Inventory Control & Auto-Expenses

  • Inventory Monitoring: Items at or below their minimum quantity are flagged as low stock.
  • Record Purchase (Restocking): Navigate to Inventory → Record Purchase, select the supplier, add restocked items with quantity and unit cost, and submit. Stock levels increase immediately, and a matching Expenditure entry is automatically logged in the Finance ledger.
  • Midnight Stock Audit: A daily automated job sweeps inventory for low-stock items and alerts admins/accountants.

2. Finance Reports (VAT / Non-VAT / Summary / Customer Dues)

Navigate to Finance Reports, which has five tabs:

  • Overview: Interactive cash flow chart, income/expenditure/net-profit KPIs, and an outstanding dues total, all filterable by a date range.
  • VAT Report and Non-VAT Report: Separate ledgers listing VAT-applicable versus Non-VAT sales and purchases, for tax filing and auditing.
  • Summary Report: A consolidated view combining income, expenditure, VAT collected/paid, and outstanding dues for the selected date range.
  • Customer Dues: Shows exactly which customers currently have an outstanding balance and how much is pending, grouped per customer with an expandable breakdown of their individual unpaid invoices. Use the Month and Year dropdown filters at the top to narrow dues to a specific billing period, or leave both on "All" to see all-time outstanding balances.
  • Add Expenditure: Manually log shop overhead costs (rent, utilities, salaries, etc.) from the Overview tab.

3. In-App Notifications

  • The bell icon in the Topbar shows a live unread-count badge, and the full Notifications page lists all alerts with Status (All/Unread/Read) and Type filters.
  • Notifications are targeted by role — e.g., accountants receive payment notifications, technicians receive servicing updates, admins see audit and low-stock alerts.

4. To-Do List Tasks (RBAC Enforced)

  • Admins, Receptionists, and Accountants can create, assign, edit, and delete tasks.
  • Technicians can only view tasks assigned to them and update the Status (Not Started / In Progress / Completed).

5. System Audit Log

  • Admins can access Audit Logs for a timestamped record of every significant creation, deletion, or modification across the system, including the acting user and a details string.

6. System Settings

  • Admins can configure global settings under Settings: garage name, phone, and address (company branding shown across invoices and receipts), the VAT rate applied to VAT-type invoices, and toggles for low-stock alerts, Day Book closure reminders, automatic WhatsApp prompts, and the loyalty points system.
  • All other roles can view these settings but cannot change them.

7. Public Landing Page

  • The public marketing site (/landing.html) showcases garage services and company information for unauthenticated visitors.
  • Clicking Login routes to /login; once signed in, staff land on the operational dashboard.

8. Daily Day Book Ledger

  • Opening & Closing Reconciliation: Tracks physical cash and bank balances day-by-day. Receipts and payments automatically compute an Expected Closing Balance reconciled against the Actual Closing Balance entered by staff.
  • Security Locks: Once a day's Day Book is closed, edits are rejected unless the requesting user has the admin role.

9. Staff Salaries & Monthly Attendance

  • Attendance Log: Admins record daily attendance (Present, Absent, Half-Day, Paid Leave).
  • Payroll Calculator: Automatically computes monthly salary based on base salary, attendance, and logged overtime hours, with payslips saved and audit-logged.

10. Loyalty Points Adjustments

  • In addition to automatic rewards (1 point per Rs. 10 paid), Admins can manually add or deduct points via the Loyalty Ledger with a mandatory reason note.

11. Nepali Calendar Dates

  • All dates and timestamps shown across the dashboard — appointments, invoices, service reminders, audit logs, and reports — are displayed in the Bikram Sambat (Nepali) calendar with English month names and prompts, so staff can read them without manual conversion.
